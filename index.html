Section A: Import Cost Parameters (Shared)
📦 What is Landed Cost?
Landed Cost is the total per-unit cost to bring goods from an overseas supplier to a Korean warehouse.
It sums: FOB price (ex-factory/ship) + freight + insurance + import duty + ancillary clearance costs. This value becomes the foundational cost input for the Value Chain (TAC) analysis in Step 3.
Trade terms for international pricing.
FOB: Seller bears costs to vessel loading — most common | CIF: Freight + insurance included (skip freight input) | DDP: Duties + VAT pre-paid by seller (skip duty input)
📌 Always use the "Send (TT Selling)" rate, not the mid-market rate.
This is the actual rate applied when remitting payment to your overseas supplier — it is slightly higher than the mid-market rate.

Step 3: SKU & Channel Setup
Part A: Channel Selection
📊 What are GP% (Retailer Margin) and Trade% (Commercial Conditions)?
GP% = The retailer's margin as a % of RSP (ex-VAT). This determines the supply price you receive.
Supply Price = RSP ÷ 1.1 (ex-VAT) × (1 − GP%)
Trade% = Additional commercial conditions beyond GP% (e.g., promotional support, logistics pass-through). Deducted from supply price and shown separately in the GM% breakdown.
※ Selecting a retailer pre-fills industry-standard values — adjust to reflect your actual contract terms
Part B: SKU Value Chain Parameters
💡 TAC (Total Acquisition Cost) Formula
TAC = Landed Cost + Packaging + Labor + Channel Logistics + Other + Vendor Fee − FOC Rebate
Logistics: % of supply price or fixed ₩ (logistics charged by channel, e.g., Coupang Rocket) | Vendor Fee%: % of supply price (dealer/agent commission) | FOC%: Free-of-charge rebate as % of Mfg Supply Price (cost deduction)
